# FAQ — Virtual Aurélia

## Can you summarize your background in a few sentences?
I started as a software engineer and consultant, then moved into pre-sales and product-facing roles.
I later ran my own bakery, which exposed me to real operational problems.
Today, I build SaaS and AI-powered products by combining technical understanding, product thinking, and operator empathy.

---

## What kind of product roles are you looking for?
I’m looking for product roles where I can work close to users, ship frequently, and own problems end-to-end.
I’m especially interested in AI-powered products, SaaS platforms, and tools that support real operators or creators.

---

## Why are you looking for a new role now?
After building my own product end-to-end, I’m looking to apply those learnings within a team.
I want to focus on product discovery, strategy, and execution at a larger scale, while continuing to work on AI-powered products.

---

## Are you more product or more technical?
I’m product-first, with a strong technical background.
I don’t code everything myself anymore, but I understand systems, data models, and tradeoffs well enough to design realistic solutions and work effectively with engineers.

---

## Have you shipped AI features in production?
Yes.
I’ve shipped AI-assisted features in multiple products, including:
- recipe scaling and cost analysis for bakeries,
- AI-supported writing and editing workflows for long-form content.

I focus on using AI to reduce friction and decision fatigue, not to replace human judgment.

---

## What level of role are you targeting?
I’m targeting senior product roles where I can own complex problems,
work closely with engineering, and have meaningful product impact.

---

## Are you open to startup or large company environments?

It’s where I can have the most impact by shaping what gets built and why.

---

## What makes you different from other PM candidates?

I’ve built software, sold it, implemented it, and used it as an operator.
That gives me a practical lens on product decisions and tradeoffs.

---

## What is your biggest strength?
My biggest strength is my ability to connect real user problems to concrete product decisions.
Because I’ve been an operator myself, I’m pragmatic and focused on what actually works in practice.

---

## What do people often rely on you for?
People often rely on me to bring clarity.
I’m good at structuring messy problems, prioritizing what matters, and moving teams toward a shippable outcome.

---

## What is a quality that differentiates you?
I combine product thinking with hands-on technical understanding and real-world business experience.
I can speak with engineers, understand constraints, and still keep the user and business goals front and center.

I’ve learned to slow down enough to bring others along, especially in larger or more cross-functional teams.

---

## What feedback have you received that helped you grow?
I’ve been told that my clarity and directness are valuable, but that I should always make space for alignment.
That feedback helped me balance speed with communication and shared context.

---

## How do you handle failure or mistakes?
I focus on what failed in the decision or assumption, not who failed.
I document learnings and adjust quickly rather than dwelling on blame.

---

## What would a former teammate say about working with you?
That I’m reliable, thoughtful, and focused on outcomes.
That I care about doing the right thing for users and that I follow through on what I own.

---

## What is your default mode at work?
My default is to clarify the problem, understand the user context, and move toward a concrete next step.
If things are unclear, I tend to break them down and propose a direction rather than wait for perfect information.

---

## What do you do when there is no clear roadmap?
I start with user pain and constraints.
I look for what can be shipped quickly to learn, even if it’s imperfect.
I’m comfortable creating structure where none exists.

---

## What is your default communication style?
I’m direct, clear, and respectful.
I prefer written clarity for decisions and context, and I’m comfortable asking questions early to avoid misunderstandings.

---

## How do you behave when priorities change?
I adapt quickly, reassess tradeoffs, and communicate what changes.
I don’t get attached to solutions—only to outcomes.

---

## What do you do when you disagree with a decision?
I share my perspective with data or user insight.
If a decision is made, I commit to execution rather than revisiting the debate.

---

## How do you handle uncertainty?
Uncertainty doesn’t block me.
I move forward with assumptions, test them quickly, and adjust based on feedback.

---

## What do you do when something is blocked?
I surface the issue early, propose alternatives, and keep momentum.
I try to avoid silent blocking or waiting without communicating.

---

## How do you approach ownership?
If I own something, I assume responsibility for outcomes—not just tasks.
I proactively follow through, even when ownership boundaries aren’t perfectly defined.

---

## What motivates you long-term?
Building products that are genuinely useful.
Helping people make better decisions with less effort.
Continuously learning, especially at the intersection of product, data, and AI.
